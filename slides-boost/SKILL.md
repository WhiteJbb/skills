# Slides Boost

Blocks the 4 causes of weak decks: slides-as-document, topic-titles, no narrative arc, filler slides. A deck is a spoken argument with visual evidence — the story exists before the first slide.

## Language
User-facing responses in Korean. Slide content follows the requested language.

## 1. Delivery contract
- Three lines: audience / the decision or action sought / minutes available.
- Slide budget = minutes ÷ 1.5, rounded down. Over budget = cut slides, not talk faster.

## 2. Storyline first (before any slide exists)
- Write the ENTIRE argument as numbered assertion sentences — this storyline is a PLANNING artifact, not the slide titles verbatim.
- Headline test (the core gate): read the assertions alone, top to bottom — they must form a complete, convincing argument with no slides attached. An assertion stating a topic ("Market overview") instead of a claim ("Mid-market grows 3x faster than we planned for") fails.
- Arc check: opening stakes (why listen) → tension (problem/gap) → resolution (your answer) → concrete ask. Each slide answers the question the previous one raises.

## 3. Build slides
- Each slide title DISTILLS its storyline assertion into a short claim (≤8 words) that still takes a side ("False pages burn 41% of on-call", not "On-call metrics"). Do NOT paste the full assertion sentence as the title (measured: full-sentence titles duplicate the body, break one-idea focus, and lost a blind judging clearly). The title claims; the body proves.
- One idea per slide: the body carries ONLY evidence for that title and never restates it. Evidence for a different point belongs on a different slide, or nowhere.
- Body ≤ 30 words or one exhibit (chart / diagram / demo). Needs paragraphs → it's a document; move prose to speaker notes or appendix.
- Numbers and quotes re-verified against sources at write time.
- Visual layer (layout, tokens, charts): follow design-boost / dataviz rules; one visual system for the whole deck, never restyled per slide.

## 4. Done gate
- Deliverable hygiene: the deck file contains only slides. The status line and process notes go in your chat response, never inside the deck.
- Re-run the headline test on the FINAL deck — titles drift during building.
- Orphan check: per slide, "if removed, does the argument break?" No → cut. Count the cuts.
- Time check: slides × 1.5 min within budget; the ask slide exists and is concrete.
- End with: `headline test: pass/fail | slides: N for M min | orphans cut: K | ask: stated`

## Never
Build a slide before the storyline · title = topic instead of claim · paragraphs on a slide · two ideas on one slide · exceed the time budget · end without a concrete ask

# Excerpt C — "Reduced working time: a review of the evidence" (meta-analysis excerpt, 2025)

Across 43 studies of reduced-hours interventions, effects are heterogeneous and moderated by work type. Strongest productivity-neutral or positive results appear in asynchronous knowledge work with high task autonomy. Coverage-constrained functions (customer support, operations, healthcare shifts) consistently show service-level degradation unless staffing rises 15-20% or coverage models are redesigned. The review flags probable publication bias: pilots run or funded by advocacy organizations report effect sizes roughly twice those of independently run studies, and null results appear underreported. Well-designed phased pilots with pre-registered service-level metrics are recommended over organization-wide switches.

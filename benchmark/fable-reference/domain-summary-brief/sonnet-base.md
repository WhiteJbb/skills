# Executive Summary — Meridian Line Expansion

Cedar Falls' Meridian line (2009) runs at 92% utilization vs. a 78% industry benchmark, leaving no maintenance slack and driving $310K/quarter overtime — deemed unsustainable beyond a year.

**Demand:** Helix Retail LOI for 2,400 tons/year (~$11.2M/year), starting Q2 2027. Caveats: Helix can exit at month 18 (90 days' notice); Danvers Mills is adding capacity (H1 2027), risking up to 7% regional price pressure.

**Cost:** New 3,100 tons/year line quoted at $18.4M (Steinmann) vs. $15.0M approved capital — a $3.4M gap needing approval. Timeline: 14 months to first production (~6 months fabrication); 60% euro-denominated, hedge recommended.

**Risks:** Wastewater permit renewal (decision March 2027) is the hard critical-path item (one comparable state denial in 5 years). Labor: 24 new operators amid 2.1% county unemployment; agency bridge costs ~$480K/year. Currency: 10% adverse FX move pre-hedge adds ~$1.1M.

**Alternative:** Northgate co-packing ($2.9M/year, -6 margin points) rejected as permanent but viable as a bridge before the new line starts.

**Financials:** Base case payback 3.8 years, IRR 16.5%. If Helix exits at month 18 unreplaced, payback stretches to 6.9 years; Danvers pricing further trims returns.

**Recommendation:** Phased release — $6.2M now for long-lead fabrication, balance deferred to the March 2027 permit decision, full release revisited at April board meeting. Approve the $3.4M gap and phasing as one package.

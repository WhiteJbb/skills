# Meridian Expansion — Executive Summary

**Constraint:** Cedar Falls' Meridian line runs at 92% utilization (78% benchmark), leaving no maintenance slack and forcing $310K/quarter overtime — unsustainable beyond a year.

**Demand:** Helix Retail's LOI commits 2,400 tons/year private-label broth (~$11.2M/year), shipping from Q2 2027. Conditions: a month-18 exit clause (90 days' notice); and Danvers Mills' H1 2027 capacity addition, expected to bring up to 7% price pressure.

**Cost/schedule:** A second line (3,100 tons/year) is quoted at $18.4M turnkey (Steinmann) vs. $15.0M envelope — a $3.4M unfunded gap. Timeline: 14 months to first production (~6 months fabrication). ~60% is euro-denominated; Treasury recommends hedging at signing.

**Risks:** (1) Wastewater permit renewal, decided March 2027 — the line cannot operate if denied (low but not negligible); sole critical-path dependency. (2) Labor — 24 operators needed amid 2.1% unemployment; agency bridge adds ~$480K/year. (3) Currency — a 10% adverse move adds ~$1.1M.

**Alternative:** Northgate co-packing ($2.9M/year, six-point margin compression) rejected as permanent, but viable as a construction-window bridge.

**Financials:** Base case payback 3.8 years, IRR 16.5%; if Helix exits at month 18 unreplaced, payback stretches to 6.9 years. Price pressure alone stays within five-year guidance.

**Recommendation:** Phased commitment — release $6.2M now for long-lead fabrication, defer the balance until the March 2027 permit decision; full release at April board meeting. Approve the $3.4M gap and phased structure as one package.
